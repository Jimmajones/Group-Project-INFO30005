About Diabetes

What is diabetes?

Diabetes mellitus, commonly referred to as diabetes, refers to a group of health conditions that result in high blood glucose levels.

What causes diabetes?

There are multiple types of diabetes with different causes. The most common types are the following:

Type 1 diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is caused by the pancreas being unable to produce enough insulin due to an autoimmune condition.

Type 2 diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is onset with an increased resistance to insulin. It is most commonly caused by excessive body weight and a lack of exercise.

Gestational diabetes

Gestational diabetes occurs during pregnancy, indicated by high blood glucose levels. Blood glucose levels generally return to normal after childbirth, but there is an increased risk of an onset of type 2 diabetes.
